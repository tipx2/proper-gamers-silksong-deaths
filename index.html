The Katok

In traditional Russian cooking, a chugun (cast-iron pot) is handled inside the oven with a long tool called an ukhvat. To make sliding heavy pots easier, a small roller called a katok is attached to the handle. It reduces friction and helps guide the pot smoothly in and out of the oven.

A traditional ukhvat with its wooden katok roller
